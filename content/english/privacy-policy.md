Food Foundations Project is a student-led educational project that provides workshops and educational materials about food literacy, nutrition basics, cooking skills, food safety, and healthy habits.

This Privacy Policy explains how Food Foundations Project, referred to here as “Food Foundations,” “we,” “us,” or “our,” handles information when someone visits our website, contacts us, requests or registers for a workshop, applies to volunteer, communicates with us as a parent or partner, or otherwise interacts with our programs.

Food Foundations is not a medical provider, healthcare provider, registered dietitian service, licensed childcare provider, or government agency. Our programs are educational. We do not provide diagnoses, treatment, medical advice, or individualized dietary advice.

## **What this policy covers&#xA0;**

This policy applies to information collected through:

* The Food Foundations website
* Website forms
* Google Forms used by Food Foundations
* Email and other direct communications
* Workshop registration and consent processes
* Volunteer and partnership inquiries
* Program records maintained through Google Drive, Google Sheets, Gmail, or similar services

This policy does not govern records collected and controlled independently by a school, museum, community center, social-media platform, website provider, or other third party. Those organizations may have their own privacy policies and responsibilities.

The information requested depends on the form and the reason for the submission.

Depending on the interaction, a form may request a name, email address, phone number, organization, role or title, subject, message, workshop request details, proposed dates and times, program location, participant age or grade range, estimated attendance, scheduling availability, staff and supervision information, parent or guardian contact information, a child’s first name and age or age range, participation permission, relevant allergy or dietary information, relevant accessibility or safety information, volunteer availability and experience, and optional media-consent choices. Not every form requests every field.

## **Information we collect**

The information we collect depends on how a person interacts with Food Foundations.

### **General inquiries**

When an adult contacts us, we may receive their name, email address, phone number, organization, role, message, and prior correspondence with us.

This information may come through a website form, email, telephone call, social-media message, or referral from a partner.

### **Workshop and partnership information**

A school, museum, community center, or other partner may provide information needed to plan a workshop. This may include:

* Organization and staff contact information
* Proposed dates, times, and locations
* Student ages or grade levels
* Estimated attendance
* Staffing and supervision arrangements
* Workshop preferences
* Accessibility needs
* Food and venue policies
* Allergy or dietary information
* Other information required by the host organization

We ask partners to provide only the information reasonably needed to organize and run the program. They should not send us full medical records or unrelated sensitive information about children.

### **Parent, guardian, and participant information**

When Food Foundations directly manages registration or consent, a parent, guardian, or other authorized adult may provide:

* Their name and contact information
* A child’s first name
* A child’s age or age range
* Scheduling information
* Permission to participate
* Relevant allergy or dietary information
* Relevant accessibility or safety information
* Media-consent choices
* Attendance or participation information

When a school or partner manages registration, Food Foundations may receive only limited program information rather than a full participant roster.

### **Volunteer information**

A volunteer inquiry or application may include:

* Name
* Contact information
* Age or age range
* School or organization
* Availability
* Relevant experience
* Areas of interest
* Résumé
* References
* Training or certification information
* Information required by a partner for site access or clearance

Sensitive identification, fingerprinting, background-check, or medical records should not be submitted through a general website form unless Food Foundations or the responsible partner has specifically approved that method.

### **Information collected automatically**

A website host or related service provider may automatically process technical information when someone visits the website. This may include an IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, approximate location, pages viewed, access times, referring page, security logs, and essential cookie information.

​

The current website at foodfoundationsproject.org is custom coded, its source code is maintained through GitHub, and it is deployed and hosted through Vercel.

Food Foundations previously used a Wix website while the current .org website was being developed. Wix is not the current host of foodfoundationsproject.org.

## **Cookies and analytics**

The website may use cookies or similar tools needed to load pages, protect the site, process forms, or remember basic settings.

Food Foundations does not currently use analytics tools to track website visitors.

​

Food Foundations does not knowingly use advertising, behavioral-tracking, or analytics cookies. Vercel and other infrastructure providers may still process basic technical information needed to deliver, secure, and maintain the website.

We do not use children’s personal information for behavioral advertising or targeted advertising.

## **How we use information**

Food Foundations may use information to:

* Respond to questions and requests
* Arrange calls, meetings, workshops, and volunteer activities
* Confirm registration and scheduling
* Prepare age-appropriate lessons and materials
* Address allergies, dietary restrictions, accessibility needs, and safety concerns
* Coordinate with parents, schools, museums, community centers, and other partners
* Maintain appropriate consent, participation, and program records
* Manage volunteers
* Meet venue or partner requirements
* Send requested information and program updates
* Conduct partnership and workshop outreach
* Protect participants, volunteers, partners, and our systems
* Investigate safety or conduct concerns
* Evaluate programs using aggregate or de-identified information
* Meet legal, administrative, or contractual requirements

Allergy, dietary, health, or accessibility information is used only for program planning and safety. It is not used to diagnose or treat a medical condition.

## **Information about children**

The general Food Foundations website and its inquiry, registration, volunteer, and partnership forms are intended for adults.

Children under 13 should not independently submit personal information through the website.

Information about a child should be provided by:

* A parent or legal guardian
* An adult authorized by the parent or guardian
* A school or program acting through an appropriate authorization or agreement
* Another responsible partner organization

When Food Foundations directly manages registration, we seek parent or guardian involvement before a child participates.

Food Foundations does not intentionally ask children to provide financial information, Social Security numbers, government identification numbers, full medical records, unrelated family information, account passwords, or personal social-media credentials.

If we learn that a child under 13 independently submitted personal information without appropriate adult involvement, we may delete the information, contact a parent or guardian, seek proper authorization, or refer the matter to the responsible school or partner.

A parent or guardian may contact us to request access to, correction of, or deletion of information about their child.

## **Registration, consent, and partner records**

Workshop registration may be handled by Food Foundations or by a host organization.

When a school, museum, community center, or other host manages registration, that organization may remain responsible for official rosters, parent permission, emergency contacts, attendance, medical or allergy records, supervision, and venue policies.

Food Foundations seeks to receive only the information needed to prepare and deliver the approved workshop.

Permission to participate is separate from optional permission to use a person’s photograph, video, voice, testimonial, name, or story.

A parent or guardian may withdraw optional media permission by contacting us. Withdrawal may not affect material already lawfully published or distributed before the request was received, but we will review the request and take reasonable steps where possible.

## **Allergy, dietary, accessibility, and safety information**

Food Foundations may receive relevant allergy, dietary, accessibility, or safety information from a parent, guardian, school, museum, community center, or authorized program partner.

We may use that information to change an ingredient, avoid a food, replace a food activity, adjust materials, coordinate with venue staff, or determine whether an activity can proceed.

Food Foundations does not use this information to diagnose or treat a condition.

We cannot guarantee that a food, ingredient, surface, facility, or third-party product is free from allergens or cross-contact.

Parents, guardians, and responsible venue staff must decide whether participation is appropriate for a particular child.

Food is served only when approved through the applicable program or venue process.

## **Photos, videos, recordings, and testimonials**

Virtual sessions are not recorded by default.

Participation in a workshop does not automatically authorize Food Foundations to use a person’s name, image, voice, recording, testimonial, or personal story.

Separate permission is required before identifiable media involving a child is used publicly. Permission may be provided by a parent, guardian, authorized adult, or responsible host organization through an appropriate process.

Declining optional media permission does not, by itself, prevent a child from participating in an otherwise available workshop.

Food Foundations team members and volunteers should not use personal social-media or messaging accounts to privately contact, tag, follow, or post identifiable information about child participants.

## **Email and outreach**

Food Foundations may send emails to respond to inquiries, coordinate programs, confirm scheduling, communicate with volunteers, follow up with partners, provide requested information, or conduct workshop and partnership outreach.

A recipient may ask us to stop sending nonessential messages by replying to the email or contacting us at the address below.

We may keep a limited do-not-contact record so that we can honor an opt-out request. We may still send messages needed for an active program, safety matter, requested transaction, or legal or administrative obligation.

Food Foundations does not currently operate a public newsletter or mailing-list signup.

## **Data retention**

Food Foundations keeps information for as long as reasonably needed to respond to an inquiry, coordinate a program, maintain appropriate consent or safety records, meet partner requirements, resolve a dispute, investigate an incident, honor an opt-out request, or maintain necessary administrative records.

Different records may be kept for different periods based on their purpose and the requirements of the host organization.

We seek to delete or de-identify information that is no longer reasonably needed.

​

Food Foundations keeps personal information only for as long as it is reasonably needed for the purpose for which it was collected. General inquiries and inactive partnership requests may be retained for up to 24 months after the last communication, while routine workshop scheduling records may be retained for up to 12 months after the related program. Child-specific allergy, dietary, accessibility, and safety information should generally be deleted or de-identified within 90 days after the program ends, unless an incident, partner requirement, or legal obligation requires longer retention. Volunteer applications, résumés, references, and training records may be kept while the person is active and for up to 24 months afterward. Consent, attendance, incident, and partner records may be kept for the period required by the responsible host organization or applicable agreement. Opt-out records may be retained as long as needed to honor the request, and duplicate, test, outdated, or unnecessary information is deleted when identified.

## **Data security**

Food Foundations uses reasonable safeguards suited to the size and nature of the project. These may include limiting access to Drive folders and spreadsheets, reviewing sharing permissions, using passwords and available multifactor authentication, avoiding unnecessary collection, separating sensitive records from general files, and deleting unneeded copies.

No method of storage or transmission is completely secure. Food Foundations cannot guarantee absolute security.

## **Access, correction, deletion, and withdrawal requests**

A parent, guardian, participant, volunteer, or other individual may contact Food Foundations to request that we:

* Explain what information we maintain about them
* Provide access to information about them or their child
* Correct inaccurate information
* Delete information that is no longer needed
* Withdraw optional media permission
* Stop nonessential communications

We may need to verify the requester’s identity, their authority to act for a child, and their relationship to the record.

Some information may need to be kept for safety, legal, contractual, administrative, or dispute-resolution reasons.

When another organization controls the record, we may direct the request to that organization.

Food Foundations does not control those services or their privacy and security practices. Their own policies apply when a person visits or uses them.
